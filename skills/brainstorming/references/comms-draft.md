# Comms Draft Template

For short-form internal communications: Slack messages, emails to teams, all-hands updates, escalation messages, post-incident comms, OTP-style rollout announcements.

The spec is short by design. A 3-5 line spec for a 3-5 sentence message.

## Sections

| Section | What goes here |
|---|---|
| Audience | Who specifically receives this? One person, a team, a channel, leadership. Be specific. |
| Intent | What do you want them to think, feel, or do after reading? |
| Key message | The one sentence they should remember. |
| Tone | Calm, urgent, apologetic, assertive, neutral. Match to context. |
| What NOT to say | Anything you're tempted to include but shouldn't. (Hedges, blame, over-explanation.) |

## Elicitation prompts

For comms-draft the elicitation is almost always batched and short. Ask all four in one message:

> "Quick frame: who's the audience, what do you want them to think or do, what's the one sentence they should remember, and what tone fits?"

Optional fifth question if context warrants:

5. Anything you're tempted to say but shouldn't? Pre-flight to remove hedges.

## Standards baked in

- **Audience specificity.** "Team" is not specific. "Engineering leads on the payments pod" is.
- **One key message.** If there are two, send two messages.
- **Tone deliberate, not default.** Choose tone before drafting, not while drafting.
- **Pre-flight what NOT to say.** Hedges, blame, premature commitments, over-explanation.
- **Short specs for short messages.** Don't engineer a 30-line spec for a 3-line Slack message.

## Anti-patterns

- Vague audiences that lead to mis-targeted tone
- Trying to communicate two things in one message
- Defaulting to "polite professional" tone when the moment calls for directness or warmth
- Burying the ask in the middle of the message
- Apologising when you shouldn't, or not apologising when you should
