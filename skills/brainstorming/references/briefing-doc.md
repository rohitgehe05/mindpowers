# Briefing Document Template

For meeting prep. Partner meetings, exec syncs, regulator briefings, stakeholder calls. The doc is for you (and your team) to walk in prepared, not for the meeting itself.

## Sections

| Section | What goes here |
|---|---|
| Meeting details | Who, when, where, format (in person, video, call). |
| Their goals | What does the other side want from this meeting? Their stated agenda and your read of their unstated agenda. |
| Our goals | What do we want from this meeting? Be specific. "Build relationship" is not a goal. |
| Topics and our positions | 3-5 topics likely to come up, and our position on each. |
| Asks (what we want) | Specific decisions, commitments, or information we want to leave with. |
| Likely objections and responses | What pushback to expect, and how we'd respond. |
| Red lines | What we will not agree to in this meeting. |
| Next steps | What needs to happen after the meeting, and who owns each. |

## Elicitation prompts

When self-shaping, one at a time:

1. Who's in the room, and what's the format?
2. What's the other side's stated agenda? What's their unstated agenda you're reading?
3. What's the one thing we need to walk out with?
4. What 3-5 topics will come up, and what's our position on each?
5. What's the strongest objection they'll raise, and how do we respond?
6. What are our red lines?

When a template match is suspected (user said "briefing for", "meeting prep", "before I see X"), batched:

> "Quick frame: who's in the room, what's the one thing you need to walk out with, what topics will come up, and what's the toughest pushback you're expecting?"

## Standards baked in

- **Their goals before yours.** Reading the other side's intent is the first move.
- **Specific asks.** "Build relationship" is not an ask. "Confirm Q3 launch date" is.
- **Anticipate objections.** Walk in with prepared responses, not improvised ones.
- **Red lines explicit.** Knowing in advance what you won't agree to prevents drift in the room.
- **Next steps owned.** Every action has a name attached.

## Anti-patterns

- Vague goals like "have a good meeting"
- Listing topics without taking a position on each
- Hoping objections won't come up
- Treating the briefing as a status report instead of meeting prep
