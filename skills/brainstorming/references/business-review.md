# Business Review (BR) Template

For weekly or quarterly product business reviews. Encodes the standard: insight before data, one idea per bullet, lowlights surfaced explicitly, parenthetical scaffolding removed.

## Sections

| Section | What goes here |
|---|---|
| Headline insight | One sentence. The most important thing this period. Lead with insight, not data. |
| Wins | 2-4 bullets. Each leads with the insight or implication, then the supporting metric. |
| Lowlights | 2-4 bullets. Surface concerns explicitly here, do not bury them in wins or "watch areas." |
| Strategic implications | 1-3 bullets. What does this period's data mean for next period or for OKRs? |
| Asks or decisions sought | 1-3 bullets. What do you need from leadership? |
| Open questions | Things still being investigated. |

## Elicitation prompts (batched)

Ask 3-4 of these in one message:

1. What's the headline insight this period? One sentence. Insight, not data.
2. Which 2-4 wins matter most? For each, what's the insight or implication? Data follows.
3. What lowlights need to surface? Don't soften, list them as lowlights, not "watch areas" or "things to monitor."
4. What strategic implication carries forward? For OKRs, next period planning, or leadership attention.
5. Any asks or decisions you need from leadership?

## Standards baked in

- **Insight before data.** Each bullet leads with the implication, then the metric.
- **One idea per bullet.** No compound bullets that mix two ideas.
- **Lowlights as lowlights.** Strategic concerns surface in their own section, not buried.
- **No parenthetical scaffolding.** Remove "(as you'll see below)", "(more on this later)", "(detail in appendix)" hedging.
- **Audience-aware tone.** Internal leadership default. Adjust if going broader.

## Anti-patterns

- Leading with data and burying the insight
- Hiding bad news in "watch areas" instead of lowlights
- Vague claims like "growth was strong" without the specific metric
- Long bullets that combine multiple insights
- Parenthetical hedging that signals uncertainty without taking a position
